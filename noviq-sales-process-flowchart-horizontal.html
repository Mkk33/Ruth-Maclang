1

Lead Intake

Goal: get every viable lead to a discovery call

Lead Sources

  • Warm inbound
  • Cold outbound
  • Referral
  • Other inbound / funnel source

Collect Before Booking

  • Property address
  • Listing links (Airbnb, VRBO, Zillow, etc.)
  • Photos
  • Ownership / decision-maker confirmation
  • Enough info to qualify against NOVIQ's ICP

Qualification Test — Must Pass All

  • Owns the property or controls the decision
  • Property is in a target market / viable use case
  • Enough upside or pain to justify NOVIQ involvement
  • Actually exploring STR / hospitality help — not just curious
2

Pre-Call Prep

Evan or Justin — before every discovery call

Pain Points to Look For

  • Bad photos
  • Missing amenities
  • Poor pricing
  • Bad reviews
  • Poor service / operational issues
  • Owner has no time
  • Underperforming revenue
  • General friction in managing the property

Rule

Do light prep. Understand visible issues. Do NOT build full underwriting upfront — otherwise the team burns time on deals that never move.
3

Discovery Zoom Call

First major conversion point — not a deck dump

Call Objectives

  • Understand their pain points
  • Confirm fit
  • Build trust and authority
  • Secure the property walkthrough
  • Gather anything still missing

Power Question

"What made you book the call today?" — reveals urgency and pain fast. (Justin's question)

Deck Usage

  • Use live during the call if helpful
  • Can send after as reference
  • Never send cold — most won't review it

Collect Before Ending

  • Property walkthrough scheduled
  • Missing property links / photos
  • Any missing business context
  • P&Ls (optional — not required yet)
4

Property Walkthrough

Second major conversion point — this is where the deal gets real

Walkthrough Goals

  • See the asset in person
  • Confirm operational and design opportunities
  • Align on key deal terms
  • Move the client toward contract signing

Align On During Walkthrough

  • Start date
  • Contract term and renewal structure
  • Management fee
  • Cleaning fees — who receives them
  • Rules / responsibilities
  • Repairs under $500: no permission needed
  • Repairs over $500: owner approval required
  • Preferred handyman / vendor structure
  • Blackout dates / owner-use restrictions
  • Buyout / cancellation provision
  • Schedule contract Zoom before leaving

Materials to Bring

Printed contract
1-page services summary
1-page cleaning / inclusions
Onboarding overview (if needed)
Do NOT show up empty-handed and vague.
5

Underwriting / Projections

Conditional — only when the deal is warm enough

When to Run It

  • After discovery, OR
  • Between discovery and walkthrough, OR
  • When the client explicitly asks for projections
Do NOT make underwriting an early bottleneck. Light prep first, full underwriting only when the deal is warm.

Asset Needed

A reusable underwriting framework slide where deal-specific numbers can be inserted each time. This was one of Evan's clearest asks.

6

Contract Review / Signing Zoom

Dedicated close call — no loose ends

Why a Dedicated Zoom

Even if they love NOVIQ after the walkthrough, clients usually want time to review the agreement. Assume this step always exists.

Walkthrough → review agreement → scheduled Zoom to sign.

Best Practice (at the walkthrough)

  • Review the contract together
  • Fill in the blanks in person
  • Give them a hard copy before leaving
  • Schedule the contract Zoom before leaving the property
7

Post-Signing Onboarding

Only after the contract is signed — now they're a client

Two Tracks

Digital onboarding ↗
Physical onboarding

Big Onboarding Deck Covers

  • What happens next
  • 30 / 60 / 90 day expectations
  • Digital onboarding steps
  • Physical onboarding steps
  • Team responsibilities
  • Launch steps

Timing

  • Usually sent after signing
  • Can be shown earlier if the owner needs more confidence to sign
